General takeaways from the data

Crime has generally been going down since the 1990s

By using polynomial linear regression, we can establish a line of best fit for the rate of total violent and property victimizations.

By changing the degree of the polynomial from 1 to 2, we can see a curved line that better fits the data. This shows that the overall crime rate dropped more precipitously in the 1990s than it has in the 21st century.

Increasing the polynomial degree also allows us to detect outliers in the data. As illustrated, 2006 and 2012 are both outlier years.

Children are often the primary victims of violent crimes

Children under the age of 18 have a higher rate of violent victimizations than of any other age cohort.

Poor people are more heavily victimized by both violent and property crime

Those with an household income of under $10,000 are more likely than any other income bracket to suffer from crime across all categories.

Next steps

To build on our application, we will employ additional data mining techniques to gain a more thorough understanding of the data. We will use association rule mining to establish relationships between crimes (ex: does being the victim of one type of crime increase the likelihood of being the victim of a different crime?).
