Additional findings using machine learning

Crime has generally been going down since the 1990s

By using linear and polynomial regression, we can establish a line of best fit for the rate of total violent and property victimizations.

By changing the degree of the polynomial from 1 to 2, we can see a curved line that better fits the data. This shows that the overall crime rate dropped more precipitously in the 1990s than it has in the 21st century.

Increasing the polynomial degree also allows us to detect outliers in the data. As illustrated, 2006 and 2012 are both outlier years.

Being the victim of one crime is a strong predictor of being the victim of another crime

By using association rule mining, we can establish the likelihood that, given an individual is the victim of one crime, they will be the victim of a different crime.

To do this, we calculated the support (the frequency with which a crime or characteristic appears in the dataset), the confidence (the strength of association between the X and Y variables), and the lift (the strength of association between X and Y, taking into account their relative frequency of occurrence). A lift value greater than 1 indicates a strong association, meaning that if an individual is the victim of X, they are more likely to be a victim of Y.

When selecting for personal characteristics rather than crimes, the lift values get lower.

As shown in these results, being the victim of one crime is a better predictor of whether an individual will be the victim of another crime more than any given personal characteristic.
